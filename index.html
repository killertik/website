About Me

My engagement with computers commenced in the mid-1980s, starting with learning Logo on school computers and Basic for playing games on the Amstrad PC, known as the Schneider PC in Europe during that period, remember the Matrix-like screens? The system operated on an 8088 chipset and used audio cassette tapes for game data storage. A bit later, someone in my street got an Atari PC with colour screen and some actual memory. We all acquired skills in system hacking to optimize the limited memory for gaming purposes. Although I did not pursue a career in computing, I have consistently stayed informed about advancements in the field and nurtured a personal interest in technology.
